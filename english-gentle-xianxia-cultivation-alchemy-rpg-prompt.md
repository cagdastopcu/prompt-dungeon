# MAIN THEME

This is a calm traveling alchemist-cultivator game set in a xianxia cultivation world.

The main gameplay loop is:

1. Search for cultivation and alchemy materials in forests, valleys, streambanks, caves, mountain foothills, or areas near villages.
2. Identify materials with the Eye of the Void.
3. Measure a material's purity, element, Qi nature, poison/healing/cultivation potential.
4. Gather it using the correct method.
5. Return to a village, town, or temporary camp.
6. Process the materials.
7. Experiment to discover new Dan pills, Lingye elixirs, poisons, antidotes, and healing products.
8. Use suitable products to support your own cultivation level.
9. Sell extra products in village, town, or city markets.
10. Take gathering, treatment, antidote-preparation, or material-delivery tasks from quest halls.
11. Travel to towns and cities to find better tools, rare materials, new customers, and recipe knowledge.
12. Quietly improve your cultivation and alchemy mastery.

This is not a game about building a market monopoly, creating a trade empire, or dominating a region.

The player may grow, open a shop, sell in cities, or become a known alchemist if they wish.  
However, the default tone is small-scale, calm, everyday, foraging-based, cultivation-based, and alchemy-for-livelihood focused.

# TRAVEL SYSTEM: VILLAGES, TOWNS, AND CITIES

The player does not have to stay in the starting village.

The player may travel to find money, materials, recipes, quests, cultivation resources, and customers.

Villages are small, calm, and low-cost.

Villages may contain:

* basic spirit herbs,
* low-level quests,
* sick or poisoned villagers,
* low-level cultivators,
* small market,
* cheap lodging,
* simple material exchange,
* local plant knowledge,
* small alchemy sales.

Profit may be low in villages, but reputation and relationships are easier to build.

## 2. Towns

Towns are livelier.

Towns may contain:

* quest hall,
* larger market,
* inn,
* blacksmith,
* herbalist,
* small alchemy shop,
* material merchant,
* more regular customer flow,
* medium-level quests,
* special orders,
* low-level cultivator communities.

Towns are main centers for selling Dan pills, Lingye elixirs, antidotes, and healing products.

## 3. Cities

Cities are more expensive but offer more opportunities.

Cities may contain:

* large markets,
* rare material merchants,
* alchemy halls,
* recipe libraries,
* wealthy cultivator customers,
* academies,
* special orders,
* high-quality tools,
* laboratory equipment,
* auctions,
* city quests,
* caravans to distant regions.

Cities can provide more money, rarer materials, and better recipe knowledge, but expenses are also higher.
